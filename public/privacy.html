Privacy Notice

How BeDeveloped handles your data inside the Base Layers Diagnostic.

Last updated 26 May 2026 · Plain-English summary

The short version

BeDeveloped runs the Base Layers Diagnostic to help your team and ours work through a structured business review together. To do that, we hold a small amount of data about you (your name, work email, the answers you give in the diagnostic) and the documents you upload. We keep everything in the UK and EU, we do not sell it, we do not use it to train AI models, and we delete it on request.

The rest of this page spells out what that means in practice. If you have any questions, email security@bedeveloped.com and we'll respond within 30 days.

What we collect

  • Your account. Work email address, the name your consultant invited you under, and the organisation you belong to. We do not see or store your password — Firebase Authentication handles that on our behalf.
  • Your diagnostic answers. The scores, comments, uploaded documents, chat messages, action items, and plan entries you create during your engagement.
  • Activity records. An audit log of privileged actions (who changed what, when, in which organisation) and counters used to spot abuse such as repeated failed sign-ins. Personal identifiers in these records are kept to the minimum we need to investigate a security incident.

We do not collect payment details, special-category data (such as health or religion), or biometric data through this tool.

Why we hold it

Everything above exists to run the engagement: showing you and your consultant the same view of the diagnostic, keeping a record of who changed what, and producing the final report. Our lawful basis is the contract between your organisation and BeDeveloped (UK GDPR Art. 6(1)(b)), backed by our legitimate interest in keeping the service secure (Art. 6(1)(f)).

Where it lives

Customer data is processed and stored in the UK and EU. Specifically:

  • Google Firebase (UK / EU). The Firestore database and Cloud Functions that power the app run in Google's europe-west2 region (London). Files you upload live in Google Cloud Storage in the same region. Authentication is handled by Google Identity Platform in its EU multi-region.
  • Sentry (EU). When something goes wrong in the browser or on our servers, an error trace is sent to Sentry's EU data centre so we can fix it. Names, emails, chat content, and comment bodies are stripped out of the trace before it leaves your browser.

Data does not leave the UK / EU under normal operation. Where a sub-processor's support staff outside the UK / EU occasionally need access (for example, Google engineers diagnosing a Firebase issue), the transfer is covered by Standard Contractual Clauses inside the underlying data-processing agreement.

How long we keep it

  • Your account and diagnostic data. Kept for as long as your organisation has an active engagement with BeDeveloped. If your account or data is deleted, it is first soft-deleted with a 30-day restore window, then permanently removed.
  • Audit log. Twelve months in our live database, then up to seven years in archive storage. We keep audit entries even after an account is erased so we can prove what happened in the event of a security investigation; the user's identifiers in those entries are replaced with an irreversible pseudonym so they can't be tied back to you.
  • Backups. Daily encrypted backups are kept for up to a year, then archived. Backups are restored only to recover from data loss.

Your rights

Under UK GDPR you have the right to:

  • Ask for a copy of your data (Art. 15). We will send you a JSON bundle of everything we hold about you across the diagnostic.
  • Ask us to delete your data (Art. 17). We will remove your account and replace your identifiers across comments, messages, and documents with a pseudonym that cannot be reversed. Audit-log entries are retained for the regulatory reasons described above.
  • Ask us to correct or restrict your data (Art. 16 and Art. 18), and to object to a specific processing activity (Art. 21).
  • Complain to the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ico.org.uk) if you think we have not handled your data properly.

To exercise any of these rights, email security@bedeveloped.com. We respond within 30 days, with a one-time extension to 60 days for complex requests (we will tell you within the first 30 days if that applies).

Security

The diagnostic requires multi-factor authentication for everyone on the BeDeveloped consulting team, encrypts data in transit and at rest, and logs every privileged action. Files you upload are restricted to a safe set of types (PDF, images, Microsoft Office documents, plain text), capped at 25 MB each, and have their contents sniffed against the declared type before they are stored. A full technical description of our controls lives in SECURITY.md in the project repository.

Contact

BeDeveloped is the controller for the data described above. Reach us at security@bedeveloped.com for data-protection questions, security disclosures, or any of the rights listed above. For the underlying technical detail referenced in security questionnaires, our compliance team maintains a more detailed technical privacy reference alongside this notice.
